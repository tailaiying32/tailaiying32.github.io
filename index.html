Experience

EmPRISE Lab

Research Assistant

I developed an active learning framework using Bayesian optimization and information theory to efficiently learn user reachability for personlized caregiving robotics. I integrated this framework with LLM-informed (e.g. CaregivingLM) priors and a learned latent user reachability space to improve the accuracy and efficiency of the active learning process. I validated the theoretical and real-world performance of the framework with over 20 ablation studies comparing query selection strategies and posterior representation methods, and deployed the framework on a Franka Emika Panda 7DOF robot arm in a 10 user study, achieving statistically significant gains in perceived human-robot engagement and user satisfaction. To support the development of this framework and the large-scale experimentation needed, I built a robust infrastructure of distributed data piplines and hyperparameter search, with countless performance optimizations such as batched processing, GPU acceleration, and distributed execution. As second author, I collaborated with a team of students, faculty, physicians, and occupational therapists to write a research paper on this project. The paper was submitted to RSS 2026 and is currently under review.
